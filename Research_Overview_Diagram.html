Concept Gauntlet
Which experience visions resonate?
2 cohorts via dScout Express: Live Data Cohort + Real but Anonymized Sample Data Cohort
4 distinct visions of the Home Surface, each expressed through two lenses. Two cohorts: Live Data (users port their own email; intelligence reflects current calibration quirks) and Anonymized Sample Data (curated to aspirational intelligence quality). Go wide, then narrow to 2–3 winners.
  • Lens 1: Landing page w/ motion — the promise. "Would I want this?"
  • Lens 2: Narrated video walkthrough — the logic. "Could I do this every morning?"
  • Divergence signal: Landing page love + walkthrough confusion = what to fix in the prototype
  • Dimension mapping: Do reactions cluster around density, framing, completion, or IA?
  • Intelligence quality signal: Cross-cohort comparison reveals how much current-state vs. aspirational intelligence quality shapes concept appeal
Feeds forward: 2–3 winning directions with refinement briefs — what worked, what to change, which dimensions matter most — plus cross-cohort comparison signal on how intelligence quality affects reception

The Home Surface depends on AI that understands your email life. Before we can test the experience, we need to know if the intelligence behind it works. A flexible toolkit of 6 methods, deployed based on what we're learning.

Experience Prototypes
Gauntlet winners built as full interactive prototypes, powered by intelligence we've already calibrated. The prototype doesn't just look right — it's backed by AI we know works. Tested by a fresh external panel who experience the integrated product directly.
Accuracy changes tolerance People accept denser surfaces when they trust the AI's priorities. Unreliable intelligence makes the same density feel overwhelming.
Framing changes trust Same accuracy feels trustworthy in a "Chief of Staff" frame but anxiety-inducing in a "Morning Brief" frame.
Completion depends on accuracy "You're all caught up" only works if the system actually caught everything. The right model differs at 70% vs. 90%.
