Track B Intelligence Calibration
Ongoing · Weeks 1-5 · 5-7 dogfooders
The big question: The Home Surface depends on AI that understands your email life — what's urgent, what's upcoming, what can wait. Before we can test the experience, we need to know if the intelligence behind it is good enough.

Method Toolkit

Six methods deployed flexibly across the study depending on what we need to learn. Shadow Inbox runs daily; others rotate in as needed.

Shadow Inbox Daily AI "report card" — simulate what the Home Surface would have shown, compare to what actually mattered. Core method.
Side-by-Side Comparison Two versions of the same inbox view. Which grouping, priority order, or summary style feels more right?
Display Variations Same content, different density/framing. Full briefs vs. summaries, cards vs. lists — what do people prefer?
Real-Time Walkthrough Live moderated session where participant narrates their inbox while we show what the AI would do. Catches mismatches in context.
Build-Your-Own Briefing Participant assembles their ideal morning view from components. Reveals what matters most when they're in control.
Stress Tests Edge cases: 200+ unread, no new mail, all promotions, mixed urgent/trivial. Where does the AI break down?
?
Priority accuracy: Does the AI's "most important" match what the person actually cares about? Target: ~80%.

Failure patterns: When it's wrong, why? Missing context? Miscategorizing? Wrong urgency read?
?
Learning curve: Does accuracy improve each day as we refine, or does it plateau?
?
Where to focus investment: Which capabilities are strong? Which need a different approach?
?
Presentation preferences: What level of detail and "done" signaling do people want?

Convergence Experience Prototypes Weeks 3-5 · Instrumented web apps
2-3 Gauntlet winners built as full interactive prototypes, powered by intelligence we've already calibrated in Track B. Tested by a fresh external panel. This is where the concept direction meets the validated AI — the prototype doesn't just look right, it's backed by intelligence we know works.
Accuracy changes tolerance People may accept a denser surface when they trust the AI is getting priorities right. If unreliable, the same density feels overwhelming.
Framing changes trust The same AI accuracy might feel trustworthy in a "Chief of Staff" frame but anxiety-inducing in a "Morning Brief" frame.
Completion depends on accuracy "You're all caught up" only works if the system actually caught everything. The right completion model differs at 70% vs. 90% accuracy.
